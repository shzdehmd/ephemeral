Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 05, 2025

Welcome to Ephemeral ("us", "we", or "our"). We are committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains our practices regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of information when you use our note-sharing service (the "Service"). By using the Service, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

1. Information We Do Not Collect

Our core principle is minimal data collection. We have designed Ephemeral specifically to avoid collecting personal information:

  • No User Accounts: We do not require you to create an account, provide an email address, or any other registration information to use the core Service.
  • No Personal Identifiers: We do not collect or store Personally Identifiable Information (PII) such as your name, address, phone number, IP address, precise location, or browser/device fingerprints in relation to your use of the note creation or viewing service.
  • No Logs: We maintain a strict no-logs policy regarding user activity. We do not keep access logs, usage logs, error logs, or any records that could link your identity or IP address to the creation or viewing of specific notes.
  • No Author Information: We do not store any information linking a created note back to its author.

2. Information We Do Collect

To provide the Service, we need to store the note itself and some functional metadata:

  • Note Content (Conditional):
    • For password-protected notes, we store the encrypted content (ciphertext) generated in your browser. We cannot read this content.
    • For unprotected notes, we store the plaintext content as entered by you.
  • Initialization Vector (IV): For password-protected notes only, we store the unique, randomly generated IV that is required (along with your password) to decrypt the note content. The IV itself does not reveal the password or the content.
  • Note Metadata: We store essential, non-personal metadata for each note, including a unique identifier, its creation time, the chosen lifespan rule (e.g., 'onetime', expiry date/time, 'permanent'), and a flag indicating if it was password-protected.

3. How We Use Information

The limited information we collect is used solely for the following purposes:

  • To Provide and Maintain the Service: Storing the note content (encrypted or plaintext) and metadata allows us to display the note to someone with the correct link and enforce its lifespan rules.
  • To Enforce Note Lifespan: We use the metadata to automatically delete notes according to the rules you set (one-time view or time-based expiry).

We do not use the collected information for advertising, analytics, profiling, or any other purpose.

4. Data Storage & Security

  • Storage Location: Note data (encrypted or plaintext, IVs, metadata) is stored on secure servers.
  • Security Measures: We implement standard technical and organizational security measures to protect the infrastructure and the stored data from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. All communication with our servers uses TLS/SSL (HTTPS) encryption.
  • Encryption (Password-Protected Notes): As detailed in our Security Details, password-protected notes use strong client-side AES-CBC encryption. Only someone with the correct link and the correct password can decrypt the content. We cannot.
  • Plaintext (Unprotected Notes): Unprotected note content is stored as plaintext and does not have the same level of confidentiality as password-protected notes.

5. Data Retention & Deletion

  • Automatic Deletion: Notes set as 'one-time view' or with a specific 'time-based expiry' are automatically and permanently deleted from our active systems once their condition is met.
  • Permanent Notes: Notes set as 'permanent' are stored indefinitely on our servers unless or until the underlying server infrastructure is decommissioned. No author information is tied to these notes.
  • No Recovery: Once a note is deleted according to its rules, it cannot be recovered.

6. Third-Party Services

We do not use third-party analytics, advertising networks, or tracking services within the core note creation and viewing functionality of Ephemeral.

7. Children's Privacy

Our Service is not intended for use by children under the age of 13 (or the relevant age of consent in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect any personally identifiable information from children.

8. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes. Changes are effective when they are posted on this page.

9. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us.
