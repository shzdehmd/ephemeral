Our Approach to Security & Privacy

At Ephemeral, we offer options for sharing information privately. Understanding how different note types are handled is key to using the service securely. This page details our security measures.

Password-Protected Notes: Client-Side Encryption

When you choose to password-protect a note, we employ strong client-side end-to-end encryption to ensure only you and the intended recipient (who has the password) can read the content.

  • Encryption Process: The encryption happens entirely within your web browser before any data related to the note content is sent to our servers. We use the standard AES-CBC encryption algorithm.
  • Your Password is Your Key: Your chosen password is used directly in your browser to generate a unique encryption key via a secure hashing process (SHA-256). Your password itself is never sent to our servers.
  • Initialization Vector (IV): For AES-CBC encryption to be secure, a unique, random Initialization Vector (IV) is generated in your browser for each encrypted note. This IV is necessary for decryption.
  • What We Store: For password-protected notes, our servers store only:
    • The encrypted note content (unintelligible data).
    • The unique Initialization Vector (IV) associated with that note (required for decryption, but does not compromise the key/password).
    • Metadata for note function (unique ID, lifespan rules).
  • Zero-Knowledge for Protected Notes: Because the encryption key is derived from your password (which we never see) and decryption requires both the password and the IV, we, the service providers, have no technical means to decrypt or read the content of your password-protected notes.

Unprotected Notes: Convenience Option

For situations where exchanging a password is inconvenient and the information is less sensitive, you can create an unprotected note. It's important to understand how these differ:

  • No Client-Side Encryption: Unprotected notes are not encrypted in your browser before being sent.
  • Plaintext Storage: The content of unprotected notes is sent to and stored on our servers as plaintext, exactly as you typed it.
  • Server Accessibility: Because they are stored unencrypted, the content of these notes could potentially be accessible to server administrators or in the event of a server compromise.
  • Recommendation: We strongly recommend using password protection for any truly sensitive or confidential information. Unprotected notes should only be used for non-sensitive content where convenience is the primary factor.

Minimal Data & No Logs Policy

Regardless of note type, we are committed to minimizing data collection and respecting your privacy:

  • No Accounts Required: The service does not require user accounts, sign-ups, or email addresses.
  • No Logging: We maintain a strict no-logs policy. We do not keep access logs, error logs, or any other logs that could correlate your activity or IP address with note creation or viewing.
  • No Personal Information Stored: We do not store Personally Identifiable Information (PII) such as IP addresses, browser details (User-Agent), location data, or any information about the creator or viewer of a note.
  • No Third-Party Tracking: We do not embed third-party tracking scripts or analytics within the core note service.
  • Stored Data Summary: Per note, we only store the functional minimum: the note content (either plaintext or encrypted blob), the IV (for encrypted notes only), a unique ID, and its lifespan rules. No data identifies the author.

Secure Note Lifespan Management

Ephemeral provides flexible control over how long your notes exist (this applies to both protected and unprotected notes):

  • One-Time View: Notes set to "One-time View" are deleted from our server after the link is successfully accessed for the first time.
  • Time-Based Expiry: You can set a specific date and time for a note to expire. Our system automatically deletes notes once their expiry time is reached.
  • Permanent Notes: Notes marked as "Permanent" are stored indefinitely on our servers until the underlying infrastructure is decommissioned or potentially removed through future administrative processes. No author information is associated with these notes. Choose this option carefully.

Secure Transmission (HTTPS/TLS)

All communication between your browser and our servers is protected using industry-standard Transport Layer Security (TLS/SSL, or HTTPS), encrypting data in transit.

Server Security

We follow standard security best practices for server configuration, maintenance, and access control to protect the infrastructure and the data stored on it (whether plaintext or encrypted).

Your Role in Security

Your choices significantly impact the security of your shared information:

  • Choose the Right Note Type: Use password protection for all sensitive information. Only use unprotected notes for non-confidential content.
  • Share Links Securely: Distribute the note link only through trusted channels.
  • Use Strong Passwords: For protected notes, use a strong, unique password.
  • Share Passwords Separately & Securely: Do not send the password in the same message as the note link for maximum security.
  • Consider "Permanent": Understand that permanent notes persist on our servers indefinitely, even if anonymously.
